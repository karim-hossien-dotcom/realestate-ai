## 1. Target Segments (Ranked by Fit)

### Tier 1 — Solo Commercial Agents, 1-5 Person Teams (Priority: Immediate)

The highest-fit segment. Commercial agents run lean operations, have no dedicated ISA staff, and handle high-value leads where a missed follow-up costs $10K+ in lost commission. They are actively shopping for automation tools, have budget authority without committee approval, and close in under 14 days once convinced. LTV is strong: at $249/month (Pro), a 24-month engagement yields $5,976. This is the primary ICP for months 0-6.

Target criteria: Licensed commercial agent, solo or team under 5, using WhatsApp for client communication (common in markets with international buyers), currently on spreadsheets, LionDesk, or nothing.

### Tier 2 — KW Commercial Offices (Priority: 30-60 days)

Nadine Khalil is a direct referral channel into KW Commercial's national network. KW's franchise structure means that a warm intro from one successful agent carries significant social proof inside the org. KW MAPS coaching scripts already embedded in the product create instant familiarity. Target office managers and team leaders at KW Commercial branches first, then expand to RE/MAX Commercial and Compass Commercial.

Approach: Nadine-narrated case study video + referral incentive ($50 credit per referred paying user). Pitch to KW Commercial office meetings as a group demo.

### Tier 3 — Displaced LionDesk Users (Priority: 60-90 days)

LionDesk was discontinued by Lone Wolf Technologies in September 2025. Tens of thousands of agents are actively evaluating alternatives. The migration window is live now. Lone Wolf's "Relationships" replacement starts at $25/month but lacks AI automation. Estate AI at $99/month positions as the AI-native upgrade. This is a time-bounded opportunity that closes as users settle into alternatives.

Capture approach: Google Ads on "LionDesk alternative" and "LionDesk replacement" keywords (estimated CPC $3-6, lower competition than generic CRM terms). Reddit r/realtors and r/CommercialRealEstate posts with honest comparisons.

### Tier 4 — International Agents, WhatsApp-Heavy Markets (Priority: 90-120 days)

UAE, Mexico, Brazil, Colombia, and Southeast Asia run WhatsApp as the primary client communication channel. Most US-built CRMs treat WhatsApp as an afterthought. Estate AI's WhatsApp-first architecture is a category-defining differentiator in these markets. CAC will be higher due to localized outreach, but churn will be lower because switching costs are higher when WhatsApp is mission-critical infrastructure.

Timing: Defer to month 4+ until the product has 20+ case studies. International sales cycles require more social proof.

### Tier 5 — FSBO-Focused Agents (Priority: Deprioritize)

High-volume, lower-commission agents who cold-prospect FSBO listings could benefit from AI outreach automation, but their willingness-to-pay is lower, their tech adoption is slower, and the segment is highly competitive (Vulcan7, REDX already own this niche). Revisit if Tier 1-3 segments plateau.

## 2. Channel Strategy

All CAC estimates assume zero brand recognition. As domain authority and case studies accumulate, these figures will decrease 30-50%.

### Channel 1 — RE Facebook Groups (Estimated CAC: $0-150)

Highest ROI for months 0-6. Follow Up Boss built early traction entirely through Lab Coat Agents (165K members) and Real Estate Mastermind (312K members). The playbook: show up with value (answer CRM questions, share AI prompt examples, post Nadine's results), not ads. One genuine post showing "this agent booked 3 meetings from WhatsApp follow-ups using Estate AI" will outperform $500 in Facebook ads.

Post cadence: 3x/week across 5 groups. Track which groups generate DMs. Convert DMs to free trials.

**Estimated monthly effort:** 5 hours. CAC: near $0 for organic, $100-200 if running targeted group ads.

### Channel 2 — Cold Email to Brokerages (Estimated CAC: $200-400)

Templates are already written. The target list: independent brokerages 5-25 agents, KW Commercial offices, RE/MAX Commercial franchises. Tools like Apollo.io ($49/mo) or Hunter.io give verified email addresses at scale.

Sequence: 3-email sequence over 14 days. Email 1: problem-aware opener about missed follow-ups. Email 2: Nadine's result as social proof. Email 3: 14-day trial offer with no credit card.

Benchmark: B2B cold email averages 1-3% reply rate, 20-30% conversion from positive reply to trial. At 1,000 sends per month, expect 10-30 replies, 5-10 trials, 2-4 new customers. CAC: $200-400 including tool costs.

### Channel 3 — LinkedIn Organic + Targeted Ads (Estimated CAC: $300-600)

LinkedIn CPA for B2B SaaS in 2026 runs $150-400 for self-serve products. RE agents are reachable via job title targeting ("Real Estate Agent," "Commercial Real Estate Broker," "KW Commercial"). LinkedIn organic posts with before/after workflow comparisons (spreadsheet chaos vs. Estate AI) build credibility over 60-90 days before ads amplify reach.

Ad format recommendation: Lead Gen Forms (convert at 15-20% vs. 4-9% for landing page clicks). Budget: $500-1,000/month to test. Expected 2-5 trials per week at scale.

**Verdict:** Start organic in month 1, run $500 ad test in month 2, scale if CAC < $500.

### Channel 4 — Google Ads (Estimated CAC: $400-800)

Generic real estate CRM keywords ("real estate CRM," "AI for realtors") carry CPCs of $3.50-5.50 and significant competition from Follow Up Boss, kvCORE, and Lofty. Budget requirement to generate signal: minimum $1,500/month. Not recommended until month 3+.

Higher-priority keyword targets with lower CPC and stronger intent:
- "LionDesk alternative" — CPC est. $3-6, high purchase intent, time-sensitive
- "real estate WhatsApp CRM" — low competition, maps to core differentiator
- "KW CRM automation" — brand-adjacent, lower CPM
- "commercial real estate lead follow up software" — long-tail, lower CPC, high intent

**Verdict:** Launch a $500/month test in month 3 targeting LionDesk migration and WhatsApp-CRM keywords. Scale based on CAC data.

### Channel 5 — Facebook/Instagram Ads (Estimated CAC: $300-700)

RE agents skew toward Facebook over Instagram. Lookalike audiences built from Nadine's contacts or email lists of RE agents allow precise targeting. Video ads showing the WhatsApp AI reply in action convert better than static images for this use case.

**Verdict:** Month 4+ after organic channels have generated enough case study content to use in ads. Budget $500-1,000/month as a test.

### Channel 6 — Partnerships — KW MAPS, MLS, Coaching Programs (CAC: near $0, long lead time)

The highest-leverage channel long-term but slowest to execute. KW MAPS coaching program partnership would put Estate AI in front of thousands of agents already using KW scripts — the exact audience the product was built for. Similar opportunity with Tom Ferry coaching programs (100K+ agents) and Craig Proctor's system.

Approach: reach out to program administrators offering revenue share (20-30% of subscription revenue for referred users) or co-branded case studies. Timeline: 60-120 days to close a partnership. Prioritize after the first 25 customers validate the product.

MLS integrations (RETS/RESO) are a year-1 enterprise play — deprioritize until month 9+.

### Channel 7 — Product Hunt Launch (Estimated CAC: $50-200, one-time spike)

A well-executed Product Hunt launch can generate 200-500 sign-ups in 24 hours. Works best when the founding story is compelling (built for one client, scaled to a product) and the timing is tight (Tuesday-Thursday launches outperform). Value is in the SEO backlink and the "as featured on Product Hunt" badge more than sustained customer acquisition.

**Recommendation:** Launch in month 2-3 once a polished demo video and 5+ testimonials are in place. Target top-5 in "Real Estate" category. Do not anchor the GTM plan to this channel — treat it as a one-time spike.

### Channel 8 — AppSumo (Recommendation: NO)

AppSumo extracts 70% of revenue (40% marketing + 30% AppSumo share), leaving founders with 30 cents per dollar. A lifetime deal at $299 one-time (typical AppSumo price for a $99/month tool) yields $90 per customer before support costs. More critically, a lifetime-deal user base creates expectation anchoring against future pricing changes and clogs support queues with bargain-hunters who churn after the honeymoon period.

**Verdict:** AppSumo is appropriate only if the product has hit a dead-end and needs a cash injection to survive. At $25.54/month burn, there is no urgency. Do not launch on AppSumo.

## 3. Pricing Strategy

### Current Positioning vs. Competitors

| Product | Entry Price | Mid Tier | Top Tier | AI Included |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Estate AI | $99/mo | $249/mo | $499/mo | Yes (all tiers) |
| Follow Up Boss | $69/mo | $499/mo | $1,000+/mo | Add-on |
| kvCORE/BoldTrail | $499/mo | $999/mo | $1,800/mo | Limited |
| Lofty (Chime) | $300+/mo | $600+/mo | $1,000+/mo | Yes |
| LionDesk (defunct) | $25/mo | $49/mo | $99/mo | No |

Estate AI is priced competitively for its AI capabilities and significantly under kvCORE and Lofty. The risk is being perceived as "too cheap" relative to enterprise alternatives — which signals limited support and scalability — versus the opportunity to undercut on price-to-value for solo agents and small teams.

### Free Tier Recommendation: No

A free tier commoditizes the AI features that justify the premium price. It also introduces support overhead for users who will never convert. Instead, offer a 14-day free trial with no credit card required. This removes friction while preserving the price signal.

### Annual Discount

The current 17% annual discount (approximately 2 months free) is below SaaS benchmarks. Industry standard is 20-25% for annual plans. Increasing to 20% (net 2.4 months free) would improve cash flow from annual prepayments and reduce churn without a significant revenue hit. Recommendation: move to 20% annual discount in month 3 when the first 10 customers validate willingness-to-pay.

### First 50 Customers Promo

Offer a Founding Member rate: $79/month (Starter) and $199/month (Pro), locked for 12 months, for the first 50 customers. This creates urgency, rewards early adopters, and generates a cohort of evangelists who feel ownership in the product's success. Promote the countdown ("37 spots remaining") in all channels. After 50, return to standard pricing.

## 4. Milestones

### 0-10 Customers (Months 1-2): Validate and Document

The entire focus is getting 10 paying customers by any means necessary and turning each one into a documented case study.

- Activate Nadine's network as a referral engine. Set up a $50 account credit per referred paying customer.
- Post daily in 5 RE Facebook groups (Lab Coat Agents, Real Estate Mastermind, Tech Support for RE Agents, New RE Agents, Realtor Networking).
- Send 500 cold emails per week to solo commercial agents using Hunter.io or Apollo.io.
- Personal outreach to 20 KW Commercial offices with a one-pager and Nadine's case study.
- Interview every new customer after their first week. Turn quotes into LinkedIn posts.
- Do not add features. Fix bugs. Improve onboarding. The product is good enough — distribution is the gap.

Revenue target: $990-2,490 MRR.

### 10-50 Customers (Months 3-6): Build Distribution Infrastructure

With 10 customers and real testimonials, shift from manual outreach to scalable distribution.

- Launch Product Hunt (month 3).
- Start Google Ads at $500/month targeting LionDesk migration and WhatsApp-CRM keywords.
- Record 5 YouTube demo videos (10-15 minutes each, screen-recorded walkthroughs of the WhatsApp AI, campaign builder, lead scoring).
- Pitch as a guest on Massive Agent podcast, Stay Paid, and Real Estate Rockstars.
- Begin partnership outreach to KW MAPS and Tom Ferry coaching programs.
- Hire 1 part-time VA ($500-800/month) to manage Facebook group posting and cold email sequences.
- Instrument churn: add in-app NPS at 30 days. Investigate every cancellation.

Revenue target: $4,950-12,450 MRR.

### 50-100 Customers (Months 7-10): Infrastructure and Retention

The product and team will show stress at 50+ customers. Infrastructure gaps (onboarding, support, billing edge cases) must be resolved before scaling acquisition.

- Build a self-service onboarding flow: interactive product tour, pre-loaded demo leads, first-campaign wizard. Target: first value in under 15 minutes.
- Add live chat support (Intercom or Crisp, ~$50/month).
- Implement a structured referral program (in-app, not just manual). Consider Rewardful ($49/month) for affiliate tracking.
- Scale Google Ads to $2,000/month if CAC < $500 from month 3-6 tests.
- Attend Tom Ferry Summit (August 2026, Anaheim) — booth or speaking slot.
- Begin conversations with 2-3 MLS organizations about data integration partnerships.
- Add team management features for Agency tier (required to convert brokerage deals).

Revenue target: $12,450-24,900 MRR.

### 100+ Customers (Month 11+): Scaling Playbook

At 100 customers and ~$25K MRR, the unit economics are clear and the growth levers are validated.

- Hire first full-time growth role (content + paid channels).
- Expand to international markets: UAE, Mexico, Colombia. Localize onboarding for Spanish-speaking markets.
- Launch white-label offering for brokerages: $499/month base + $99/month per agent seat over 5. This unlocks the $5,000-15,000 ACV deals that transform the revenue trajectory.
- Pursue Inman Connect speaking slot (Jan 2027 NYC) — positions Estate AI as a market leader, not a challenger.
- Raise a pre-seed round ($500K-1M) if MRR growth rate exceeds 20% month-over-month for 3 consecutive months.

Revenue target: $24,900+ MRR growing 15-20% month-over-month.

## 5. Metrics to Track

All metrics logged weekly. Review monthly against benchmarks.

| Metric | Definition | Target (Month 6) | Target (Month 12) | Benchmark |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| MRR | Monthly recurring revenue | $5,000 | $25,000 | — |
| CAC | Total marketing spend / new customers acquired | < $400 | < $600 | $400-900 for RE SaaS |
| LTV | Avg monthly revenue x avg customer lifetime (months) | $2,988 (12 mo) | $4,980 (20 mo) | — |
| LTV:CAC | LTV divided by CAC | > 5:1 | > 6:1 | Industry min: 3:1 |
| CAC Payback | Months to recover CAC | < 4 months | < 3 months | SaaS benchmark: 12-18 mo |
| Monthly Churn | Customers lost / total customers | < 5% | < 3% | SaaS avg: 3-7% |
| MRR Churn | MRR lost / total MRR | < 4% | < 2% | SaaS avg: 2-5% |
| NPS | Net Promoter Score (in-app survey at day 30) | > 40 | > 50 | SaaS avg: 31 |
| Trial-to-Paid | % of trials converting to paying customers | > 20% | > 25% | SaaS avg: 15-25% |
| Activation Rate | % of new users sending first campaign within 7 days | > 50% | > 60% | Product-specific |
| ARPU | MRR / total active customers | > $180 | > $200 | Mix of Starter + Pro |

The single most important leading indicator is trial activation rate. A user who sends their first campaign within 7 days is 4x more likely to convert and 3x less likely to churn in month 1. Every onboarding decision should optimize for this event.
