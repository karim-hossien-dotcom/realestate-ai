## 3. MLS Technology Partner Programs — How They Work

### The General Model

MLSs do not run a single unified certification program. Each MLS independently decides which technology vendors to approve. The typical path has three tiers:

**Tier 1 — IDX/Data Feed Approval**
The most common entry point. A vendor applies to receive an IDX or RESO API data feed. This allows the vendor to display MLS listings on their platform and is the foundation for any MLS data integration. Most MLSs distribute data through aggregators:
- **CoreLogic Trestle** — used by CRMLS, Bright MLS, NTREIS, and many others. Single contract grants access to multiple MLSs. Entry point: [treste.corelogic.com](https://trestle.corelogic.com)
- **MLS Grid** — used by OneKey MLS, GSMLS, and others. Aggregates 200+ MLSs under one RESO-compliant API. Entry point: [mlsgrid.com](https://mlsgrid.com)

**Tier 2 — Preferred/Approved Vendor Listing**
Some MLSs maintain a curated list of "approved" or "preferred" technology vendors promoted to their member agents. Getting listed here is a marketing channel in itself. Examples: Bright MLS's approved IDX vendor list, OneKey MLS's Technology Vendor Benefits page.

**Tier 3 — Featured/Endorsed Partner**
A small number of MLSs create deeper partnerships with select tech companies — co-branded promotions, inclusion in agent onboarding, or revenue-sharing models. This typically requires proven integration and a track record of adoption.

### Fees Observed
- Bright MLS IDX feed: $7,500/year + $200/brokerage over 20
- Trestle API: Pricing negotiated per contract (volume-based)
- MLS Grid: Licensing fees vary by MLS and data volume
- OneKey MLS vendor program: fees not publicly listed — must apply

### AI Tools Getting MLS Certification (Recent Examples)
- **Infinityy** — AI-guided property/neighborhood experience platform; selected for NAR REACH 2025
- **NEO** — Multilingual agent sales platform; selected for NAR REACH 2025; partners with associations and MLSs
- **Ocusell** — Multi-MLS listing platform; formal partnership with Bright MLS announced Sept 2025
- These examples confirm that AI-native products are actively being certified by MLSs right now

## 4. Commercial-Specific Platforms — CoStar, LoopNet, Crexi

These are **not MLSs** in the traditional NAR-affiliated sense. They are commercial listing marketplaces. The distinction matters:

| Platform | Type | Partnership Availability |
|----------|------|--------------------------|
| **CoStar** | Subscription data platform (owns LoopNet, Apartments.com) | API access via negotiated enterprise licensing only. Not startup-friendly. Very restrictive on data sublicensing. Requires strategic alignment. |
| **LoopNet** | CoStar subsidiary — commercial listing marketplace | No independent API; bundled under CoStar enterprise contracts |
| **Crexi (CREXi)** | Independent commercial real estate marketplace | Most accessible. Offers a self-service Listing API. Launched Integrations Hub (beta) in 2025 with Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive, Zoho, and Follow Up Boss connectors |

**Crexi is the recommended commercial platform partner.** Their 2025 year in review confirmed expanded MLS data integrations bringing 327,038+ active commercial listings onto the platform. Their Integrations Hub already connects to Follow Up Boss — which means Estate AI could position itself as the next CRM integration.

- **Crexi integrations contact:** integrations@crexi.com
- **Crexi Listing API docs:** [learn.crexi.com/listing-api-overview](https://learn.crexi.com/listing-api-overview-crexi-help-center)

CoStar should be approached only after achieving market traction, given their restrictive licensing posture and ongoing antitrust litigation with Crexi.
