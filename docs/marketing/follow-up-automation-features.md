# Follow-Up Automation — Marketing Reference
**Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** April 14, 2026 | **Audience:** Sales conversations, blog content, ad copy

---

## The Problem

Most real estate agents quit following up after 3-5 touches. A 400,000-lead study by Greg Harrelson found that **80% of conversions happen after touch 6** — yet the industry average agent stops at 5. That gap is where deals die.

The problem isn't motivation. It's systems. Manual follow-up is inconsistent, agents forget, and generic CRM sequences treat every lead the same whether it's a cash buyer ready to move in 30 days or a seller who won't list for 8 months.

The result: agents chase hot leads, abandon warm ones, and leave a trail of dead-but-convertible leads behind them.

---

## Our Solution

Estate AI's follow-up automation is built around three capabilities no competitor combines:

### 1. Research-Backed 8-Touch Sequences (and beyond)

Four pre-built cadence templates, each designed for a different sales timeline:

| Template | Touches | Duration | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| Aggressive | 8 touches | 21 days | Hot buyers, urgent leads |
| Standard | 6 touches | 45 days | General inbound leads |
| Gentle | 5 touches | 90 days | Seller nurture, long-cycle leads |
| Long Haul | 12 touches | 12 months | Investor lists, cold pipeline |

Every template is calibrated around the Harrelson study's finding that **95% of eventual conversions have at least one touchpoint after the 6th contact**. Estate AI doesn't stop at 3.

### 2. Per-Lead-Type Branching

The system automatically assigns the right cadence based on lead type on import — no manual setup per lead:

- **Buyers** → Aggressive 8-touch (urgency is high, window is short)
- **Sellers** → Gentle 90-day (trust-building cycle, no pressure)
- **Investors** → Long Haul 12-month (relationship play, patience required)
- **Tenants** → Short cycle (fast decision, low-friction)

Agents can override any lead at any time. But the default is smart, not generic.

### 3. Three Automation Modes

Different agents work differently. Estate AI matches your style:

- **Full Auto** — System sends on schedule. Zero intervention needed. Best when you're on showings, traveling, or slammed.
- **Approval Required** — Every message sits in a review queue before sending. You approve or edit with a configurable window (1 hour to 24 hours). Best for high-value leads where personal touch matters.
- **Manual** — Cadence tracks progress and prompts you, but you write and send each message. Full control, zero automation.

Mode can be set globally or per individual lead.

### 4. TCPA Compliance Built In

Every outbound message runs through:

- **Timezone detection** — Lead's local time calculated from area code and profile data
- **Quiet hours enforcement** — No messages sent between 9pm-8am in the lead's local timezone
- **State-specific overrides** — Florida and Oklahoma have stricter quiet hours laws (enforced automatically); more states added as regulations update
- **Full audit log** — Every message attempt logged with timestamp, timezone, and consent status

## Use Cases

**Hot Buyer, 30-Day Timeline**
Import as Buyer type. System assigns Aggressive 8-touch. Day 1: WhatsApp intro. Day 3: SMS with listing match. Day 7: Email with market data. Continues through day 21 across all three channels. Agent runs Full Auto mode, reviews reply notifications only.

**Long-Cycle Seller, 8-Month Horizon**
Import as Seller type. System assigns Gentle cadence. Touches spaced 2-3 weeks apart over 90 days — no pressure, value-add content only. Agent uses Approval Required mode to personally review each message before it sends.

**Investor List, Cold Pipeline**
Import 50 investor contacts. System assigns Long Haul 12-month cadence. Low-frequency touches (monthly) that keep Estate AI top of mind without burning the relationship. Agent runs Full Auto; checks in quarterly.
